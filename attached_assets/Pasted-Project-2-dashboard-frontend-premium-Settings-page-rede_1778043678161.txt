Design goal:
A calm, premium, customer-facing Settings experience.

Principles:
- Clear categories
- No clutter
- No technical/debug content
- Customer-friendly wording
- Mobile-first
- Desktop polished
- White/soft grey layout
- Subtle borders
- Clear section titles
- Good spacing
- No over-designed cards
- No random toggles floating in space
- No dense developer-looking rows

Settings should feel like:
“Here is where I manage my AI assistant and business information.”

Not:
“Here is a pile of technical controls.”

Required page structure:

Desktop layout:
- Page title: Settings
- Short subtitle: Manage your workspace, AI information, channels, and alerts.
- Use a two-column layout if screen width allows:
  Left side: category navigation / section list
  Right side: selected settings content
- If two-column is too much for current app, use grouped cards with clear category headers.
- Keep it simple. Do not create a complex router if unnecessary.

Mobile layout:
- Single column.
- Collapsible or stacked category cards.
- No horizontal scrolling.
- No fixed bottom nav.
- Comfortable touch targets.
- Clean spacing.

Recommended categories:

1. Workspace
Purpose: Business identity and account basics.
Contains:
- Business name
- Contact email
- Phone number
- Website
- Logo upload / preview
Customer copy:
“Manage the basic details shown in your Unboks workspace.”

2. Your Info
Purpose: Information the AI uses to answer customers.
Contains:
- Existing Your Info / knowledge cards
- Your Info Updates
- Temporary notes, offers, holidays, opening hours, seasonal info
Customer copy:
“Add information your AI should know when replying to customers.”

3. Channels
Purpose: Show/hide channels in the dashboard.
Contains:
- WhatsApp
- Instagram
- Facebook
- Messenger
- Email
- X
- TikTok
Customer copy:
“Choose which channels appear in your dashboard.”

Important:
This is about dashboard visibility, not connecting channels.
Do not imply channel integration setup unless backend supports it.

4. Escalation Alerts
Purpose: Where urgent human-needed escalations should be sent.
Contains:
- Email mandatory/default
- Optional WhatsApp
- Optional Messenger
- Optional Telegram
- Destination inputs for optional channels
Customer copy:
“Choose where urgent escalation alerts should be sent.”

5. Labels & Preferences
Purpose: Small UI preferences.
Contains:
- Orders/Bookings label setting
- Email reply preference if still needed
Customer copy:
“Customize how your dashboard looks and opens replies.”
