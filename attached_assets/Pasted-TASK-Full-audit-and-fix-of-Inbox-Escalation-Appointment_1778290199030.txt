PROBLEM:
The dashboard is putting conversations into Appointments too early.

Example:
Customer asked for an intake call.
Marina asked for 2 or 3 times.
Customer replied with:
- tomorrow evening 17:00
- Monday morning 11:00

The dashboard shows two confirmed Calvin appointments:
- Monday 11:00
- tomorrow evening 17:00

This is wrong.

The customer only gave possible time slots.
The operator did NOT choose one.
The customer did NOT confirm one selected option.

Therefore this should be:
- one Escalation
- with two proposed options
- not two confirmed appointments

CORE PRODUCT LOGIC:

Inbox:
Normal customer conversation handled by Marina.

Examples that stay in Inbox:
- customer asks a normal question
- customer asks about pricing
- customer says they want to book
- customer says they want an appointment
- customer says they want an intake call
- Marina asks follow-up questions
- Marina asks for 2 or 3 time slots

Escalation:
Human/operator decision needed.

Examples that become Escalation:
- customer gives time slots
- customer gives booking/appointment options
- customer gives availability
- customer asks for something requiring human approval
- customer asks for a human
- Marina cannot decide based on business rules
- availability/team confirmation is needed

Appointment / Booking / Order:
Only after a real outcome exists.

Examples that become Appointment:
- customer confirms the selected time
- operator explicitly confirms the appointment as final
- business rules clearly allow Marina to confirm automatically
- team/operator selected a time and customer agreed

DO NOT create an Appointment just because:
- customer wants an appointment
- customer gives possible slots
- Marina says “I’ll pass this to the team”
- Marina says “the team will confirm”
- the conversation mentions a date or time
- there are multiple proposed times

CRITICAL RULE:
Customer-proposed time slots = Escalation.
Operator-selected + customer-confirmed slot = Appointment.

SPECIFIC BUGS TO FIND:

1. Appointments created from proposed slots
If the customer says:
“tomorrow evening 17:00 and Monday morning 11:00 will work for me”

This must NOT create two appointments.

Correct classification:
One escalation with proposedTimes:
[
  "tomorrow evening 17:00",
  "Monday morning 11:00"
]

2. False confirmed status
Do not mark status as confirmed unless:
- customer confirms the selected slot
- or operator explicitly marks the appointment final

Bad:
Customer gives options → confirmed

Good:
Customer gives options → escalation
Operator chooses option → pending_customer_confirmation
Customer confirms → confirmed appointment

3. Duplicate Calvin appointments
If two rows come from the same conversation and represent alternative slots, do not display them as separate confirmed appointments.

Deduplicate by:
- conversationId
- conversationKey
- phone
- thread_key
- email thread key
- source conversation id

Only show multiple rows for the same customer if they are truly separate appointments from separate confirmations.

4. Appointment detector too aggressive
If the frontend detector scans conversation text and sees times, it must not immediately produce confirmed appointments.

Times alone are not appointments.
Times are proposed options until confirmed.

5. Sidebar count wrong
Appointments count must count real active appointments only.
Do not count proposed slots as appointments.

Count these:
- confirmed
- pending_customer_confirmation if a specific operator-selected slot exists
- pending_team_confirmation if operator/team selected a specific slot

Do not count:
- raw proposed times
- detected scheduling intent without selection
- escalation-only rows
- cancelled
- completed

6. Escalation missing
If customer gave multiple time slots and no operator decision exists, the row should appear in Escalations, not Appointments.

DEFENSIVE FRONTEND CLASSIFICATION:

If backend returns appointment rows that look too early, frontend must defensively downgrade or flag them.

If appointment row has:
- status confirmed
but source conversation only contains customer proposed slots and no operator/customer confirmation,
then do not show as confirmed appointment.

Instead:
- hide from Appointments
or
- show as “Needs operator decision” in Escalations if frontend has enough data.

Do not silently present false confirmed appointments.

CLASSIFICATION STATE MACHINE:

Use this model:

Stage 1:
customer_intent
Customer wants appointment/booking/intake.
Location: Inbox.

Stage 2:
availability_requested
Marina asked for times/details.
Location: Inbox.

Stage 3:
customer_proposed_slots
Customer gave one or more possible times.
Location: Escalations.
Status: needs_operator_decision.

Stage 4:
operator_selected_slot
Operator selected one time/location.
Location: Escalation.
Status: pending_customer_confirmation unless operator said final.

Stage 5:
customer_confirmed_slot
Customer confirmed the selected time.
Location: Appointments.
Status: confirmed.

Stage 6:
operator_final_confirmation
Operator explicitly confirmed final booking without needing customer confirmation.
Location: Appointments.
Status: confirmed.

APPOINTMENT STATUSES:

Allowed statuses:
- detected
- needs_operator_decision
- pending_team_confirmation
- pending_customer_confirmation
- confirmed
- cancelled
- completed

Frontend display rule:
Appointments page should primarily show:
- pending_customer_confirmation
- pending_team_confirmation
- confirmed

Escalations page should show:
- needs_operator_decision
- unclear scheduling choice
- multiple proposed slots awaiting operator choice
