Product problem:
The Escalations page is currently conversation-first.
When an operator clicks an escalation, they mostly see the full message trail.

That is wrong for escalation handling.

Escalations must be decision-first.

The operator needs to instantly understand:
1. Why was this escalated?
2. What does the customer want?
3. What does Marina need from the operator?
4. What are the operator’s options?
5. What should the operator reply?

The full conversation trail should still be available, but it should be secondary.

Use Refero/Repro:
Use real premium customer-support escalation/ticket references before designing.
References should include:
- Intercom conversation details
- Zendesk ticket summary panels
- Front shared inbox
- Linear issue detail summary/action panels
- Salesforce service console
- Slack thread summary/action UX

If Refero/Repro is not connected, stop and report clearly before editing.

Required new layout:
When an escalation is selected, show a premium “Escalation reason” panel at the top of the detail pane, before the message trail.

This panel must be the first thing the operator sees.

Panel title:
Escalation reason

Panel content should include:

1. Reason / Summary
Short human-readable explanation of why this escalation needs attention.

Example:
“Calvin wants to schedule an activation meeting and gave 2 possible time slots. Marina needs a human to confirm which time works.”

2. What the customer wants
Short summary of the customer request.

Example:
“Customer wants to meet this week to activate the service.”

3. What Marina needs
Specific operator decision needed.

Example:
“Choose one of the proposed time slots, suggest another time, or ask Marina to collect more availability.”

4. Operator options
Show compact action chips or bullets:
- Confirm a proposed time
- Suggest another time
- Ask Marina to collect more availability
- Switch to human takeover

5. Mode indicator
Show whether this is:
- AI needs help / Soft escalation
- Human takeover / Hard escalation

Soft escalation behavior:
- The operator replies to Marina.
- The reason panel should help the operator write guidance quickly.
- Composer title:
  Reply to Marina
- Helper:
  Marina will use your guidance to answer the customer.

Hard escalation behavior:
- The operator replies directly to the customer.
- The reason panel should explain why human takeover is needed.
- Composer title:
  Reply to customer
- Helper:
  This reply will be sent directly to the customer.
- AI Editor should remain available only in hard mode.

Message trail:
The full conversation trail must still be available, but it should not dominate the initial view.

Preferred UX:
- Put “Conversation trail” below the escalation reason panel and composer.
- It can be collapsed by default or shown lower down.
- If collapsed, button text:
  Show conversation
  Hide conversation
- If not collapsed, still place it below the reason/action panel so the operator sees the reason first.

Important:
Do not remove the conversation trail completely.
The operator should always be able to review it if needed.
